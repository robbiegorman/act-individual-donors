Purpose

A working page for alignment, not a final Salesforce build

This page is meant to help ACT communicate and organize early thinking around Salesforce updates for Individual Donors and Event Management. It is not intended to represent the current system or prescribe the final architecture. It is a practical starting point for shared discussion across Marketing + Development, Finance, Operations, and the data team.

Build shared language

Translate past conversations, slide decks, and field lists into one common resource for discussion.

Support donor development

Create a clearer view of donor history, relationship context, cultivation strategy, and follow-up.

Connect event work

Treat events as part of relationship management, not stand-alone activities disconnected from Salesforce.

Clarify integrations

Call out where Salesforce may need to connect with QuickBooks, Give Lively or another platform, Mailchimp, Stripe, United Way records, and future finance systems.

System snapshot

Current assumptions to pressure-test with the data team

The current working assumption is that Mailchimp is the primary marketing platform, events are still TBD but may lean toward Give Lively or another giving/event platform, and QuickBooks is currently used for finance. That may change in the next year or so as ACT moves toward stronger fund accounting.

Important caveat: This page is speculative. It combines the uploaded field spreadsheet with the M+D Salesforce update document to show what the future system might need to account for. It does not mean these fields or integrations currently exist in Salesforce.
Design intention: Salesforce should become the relationship and coordination system. QuickBooks should remain the accounting source of truth while it is in use. Mailchimp should support marketing engagement. Give Lively or another platform may capture online gifts, registrations, recurring gifts, and event-related activity.
Individual donors

What the donor system should help ACT do

ACT’s donor system should create one common location for donor information, reduce reliance on staff memory, build a shared history of donor engagement, and make relationship context visible over time.

Common location

Bring donor information into one shared system rather than spreading context across finance records, staff memory, spreadsheets, and emails.

Relationship history

Capture meaningful interactions, notes, key context, ACT affiliation, and engagement beyond gifts.

Ownership + handoffs

Clarify who is responsible for donor management and when handoffs should happen across staff or departments.

Common metrics

Standardize important donor fields and measures so teams can work from a shared language.

Event management

Why events need to be part of the Salesforce conversation

The M+D update makes clear that events are not just logistics. They are also donor engagement, audience segmentation, sponsorship, revenue tracking, marketing reach, day-of coordination, and follow-up.

Audience + attendance

  • Audience segments
  • Invitees, registrants, and attendees
  • Follow-up lists
  • Prior event history

Revenue + finance

  • Revenue goals
  • Sponsorship performance
  • Ticket sales
  • Expenses and budget-to-actuals

Coordination + follow-up

  • Shared event status view
  • Day-of preferences and notes
  • Vendor documents such as W-9s
  • Post-event relationship-building

Integration design

How the core systems should be understood

The purpose of calling out integrations is not to overbuild now. It is to make sure the Salesforce design does not ignore the data that may need to move between development, finance, events, and marketing.

Working flow

A simple way to think about the data movement

This is a conversation starter, not a technical integration specification.

1

Supporter activity

Gift, registration, attendance, sponsorship, volunteer interest, board referral, or partner connection.

2

Salesforce relationship record

Donor profile, ACT connection, campaign or event history, relationship owner, and follow-up.

3

Finance reconciliation

Transaction amount, deposit, fee, fund, restriction, accounting code, and QuickBooks reference while applicable.

4

Engagement loop

Thank-you, impact update, event follow-up, marketing segment, invitation, or next donor conversation.

Open questions

Discussion prompts for ACT and the data team

These questions are intended to help ACT decide what should happen next without assuming the final Salesforce architecture.

Current data

Which donor and event data sources should be reviewed first: QuickBooks exports, Give Lively or platform data, United Way records, Mailchimp, spreadsheets, or staff knowledge?

Phase 1 scope

Which fields are truly needed for a first build, and which should wait until staff process and reporting needs are clearer?

System ownership

Which system should own each data point, and what should only be referenced or synced when necessary?

Events platform

What does ACT need from an events platform before deciding whether Give Lively or another tool is the right fit?

Finance transition

How should the design account for QuickBooks now while leaving room for future fund accounting changes?

Shared process

How should Resource Development, Finance, Operations, and Marketing + Development work together when donor or event records cross departments?

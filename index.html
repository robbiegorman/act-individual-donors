Purpose

Why this donor system matters

The goal is to create a more connected, strategic, and useful view of individual donors across fundraising, finance, marketing, and events. Salesforce should become the primary home for donor relationship management, while QuickBooks remains the source of truth for accounting and financial reconciliation.

Understand donors

See who donors are, what motivates them, and how they are connected to the organization.

Manage next actions

Turn donor records into a living system for follow-up, stewardship, and asks.

Create clean data

Reduce scattered spreadsheets and design a structure that can support reporting over time.

Support finance

Align gifts, restrictions, batches, and transactions with QuickBooks without duplicating the accounting system.

Current snapshot

Where the organization appears to be in developing the system

This section gives the data team a shared starting point. It should be refined as current exports, spreadsheets, and system owners are reviewed.

Current-state assumptions

  • Individual donor information may currently live across spreadsheets, QuickBooks, donation tools, email lists, and staff knowledge.
  • QuickBooks likely contains useful financial history but should not become the primary donor relationship system.
  • Salesforce should be designed intentionally, not simply as a copy of old spreadsheets.
  • Mailchimp and donation platforms should feed useful data into Salesforce without becoming competing databases.

Salesforce owns the relationship

Donor identity, cultivation strategy, stewardship, campaign history, and next actions.

QuickBooks owns accounting truth

Deposits, revenue classification, reconciliation, transaction records, and financial reporting.

Mailchimp supports communication

Subscription status, email engagement, audience segments, campaign tags, and marketing activity.

Donation platforms feed activity

Online gifts, recurring donations, payment processor IDs, donor-entered details, and campaign forms.

Design principles

What each system should and should not do

The cleanest design keeps each system focused. Salesforce should answer fundraising and relationship questions. QuickBooks should answer accounting and reconciliation questions.

Salesforce should answer: “Who should we cultivate, why do they care, what should we ask for, and what should happen next?”
QuickBooks should answer: “Was the money received, classified correctly, deposited correctly, and reconciled?”
Data flow

Recommended system architecture

The system should allow online and offline gifts to move through a consistent path while preserving clear ownership of donor relationship data and accounting data.

1

Donor activity

Online gift, check, event attendance, referral, or staff interaction.

2

Salesforce record

Donor identity, campaign, gift, relationship owner, and stewardship workflow.

3

QuickBooks reconciliation

Transaction amount, fund, revenue category, deposit batch, and accounting record.

4

Communication loop

Mailchimp segments, impact updates, acknowledgments, and future engagement.

Implementation phases

Phase 1 versus future build

Prioritization prevents the project from becoming too large too quickly. Phase 1 should focus on clean records, gifts, acknowledgments, and reconciliation before moving into more advanced cultivation intelligence.
